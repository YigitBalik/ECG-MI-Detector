What is Interpretable AI?

Interpretable AI refers to the ability of an artificial intelligence system to provide easy-to-understand explanations of the prediction outcomes for humans. In medicine, interpretable AI is important because it can help improve the accuracy and reliability of medical diagnoses, treatment recommendations, and patient outcomes. Interpretable AI can provide doctors and other healthcare professionals with insights into how the AI system arrived at a particular diagnosis or treatment recommendation. This can help doctors make more informed decisions, verify the accuracy of the AI's output, and identify potential errors or biases in the AI system. Additionally, interpretable AI can provide patients with a better understanding of their medical conditions and treatment options, which can improve patient engagement and satisfaction. To build our application we have developed a black box convolutional neural network model. However, using a gradient class activation map (GradCAM) [1], we are able to explain the most important parts or features of the given data.

How to Use?

The primary concern of our application is to classify electrocardiogram (ECG) signals whether they belong to a healthy person or to a patient with myocardial infarction (MI). Additionally, this application is able to explain why the given signals indicate a healthy person or MI patient using GradCAM.
Step-by-step usage:
  1. Upload your ECG report in PDF format: An ECG report consists of 5 pages that contain 12-lead ECG signals. Each page contains the same signals but with different durations. Our model works on signals with a 10-second duration (pages: 3,4). Moreover, each page may contain relevant information about the patient such as age and gender. If such information is present in the report, our model also uses them.
  2. Obtain the result: The result contains the classification results and interpretations of the signals. Interpretations is a color map of the signals where hot colors (red) indicate the most important segments and cold colors (blue) indicate the non-discriminative segments.

What Happens to My Data?

All extracted data and ECG reports are being deleted permanently after the results generated.
